# Tier Classification for Axelerant Content Engine

Classify every engagement story into one of three tiers before any content is produced. Tier determines what gets created, how much effort is invested, and how the story is distributed.

## The Three Tiers

### Tier 1 — Transformational Impact Stories

Tier 1 stories have the most amount of impact. These are big problems being talked about, with descriptions of how the team comes together to solve them. It is the big picture — an example of how Axelerant brings transformational change to client projects.

**What Tier 1 requires:**
- A clear, significant, externally-relatable problem
- A credible and non-obvious approach — the team's thinking must be visible
- Sufficient technical or strategic nuance to be educational for the ICP
- Meaningful external relevance — readers outside the project learn something
- Evidence confidence score of 7 or above
- Enough content to sustain a 1,500+ word blog with substance throughout

**What Tier 1 produces:**
- Full long-form blog (1,500+ words)
- Complete distribution strategy
- All channel assets (LinkedIn, newsletter, sales enablement, campaign, platform POC)

### Tier 2 — Component-Level Insight Stories

Tier 2 stories are interesting, but there is not enough content or detail to warrant an entire article. A Tier 2 story can be a component of a Tier 1 — for example, if a Tier 1 story covers three different components involved in solving a problem, a Tier 2 story picks up any one of those components and goes deeper.

Another way to think about it: if a Tier 1 story is an itinerary for an entire 10-day vacation, a Tier 2 story is a short sightseeing trip incorporated into it.

**What Tier 2 requires:**
- A genuine insight or useful learning
- Enough evidence to be credible (confidence 5-6)
- External relevance to at least one specific ICP persona
- Clear connection to a specific challenge or decision

**What Tier 2 produces:**
- A focused distribution narrative (800-1,200 words) or deep-dive asset
- Focused activation plan for one core insight channel

### Tier 3 — Small Victory and Culture Stories

Tier 3 stories are small snippets. These celebrate small victories, highlight day-to-day workings, and reflect company culture. This is a much more zoomed-in version of the Tier 1 story.

**What Tier 3 requires:**
- A real, specific signal (not generic)
- A clear, human moment: a win, a lesson, a milestone, a culture beat
- Suitability for social — short enough to tell in a post

**What Tier 3 produces:**
- A LinkedIn or social post (150-300 words)
- 2 variants with different hooks

## Tier Ratios

For every 10 Tier 3 stories, expect approximately 5 Tier 2 stories and 1-2 Tier 1 stories.

Use this as a calibration guide. If you are classifying too many stories as Tier 1, you are probably overstating impact. If everything is Tier 3, you are probably undervaluing the material.

## Classification Decision Rules

Always justify the tier in plain language. Two questions to ask:

1. **Is the problem big enough and the outcome clear enough?** If yes for both → Tier 1 candidate. If yes for one → Tier 2 candidate. If neither → Tier 3.

2. **Would a reader outside this project learn something actionable?** If yes at depth → Tier 1. If yes narrowly → Tier 2. If it is more of an observation → Tier 3.
