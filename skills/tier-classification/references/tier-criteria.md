# Tier Classification — Detailed Criteria

## Tier 1 Checklist

Score the story against all criteria before selecting Tier 1. All items marked [REQUIRED] must be present.

| Criteria | Required? | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Clear, significant problem statement | REQUIRED | The problem must be non-trivial and externally relatable |
| Credible, non-obvious approach | REQUIRED | The team's thinking must be visible — not just "we used Drupal" |
| Measurable or strongly implied outcome | REQUIRED | Can be quantitative (30% improvement) or qualitative (structural change) |
| Sufficient technical or strategic nuance | REQUIRED | A reader must learn something real — not generic best practices |
| External relevance to Axelerant's ICP | REQUIRED | Would a CTO, CDO, or digital VP at a target account care? |
| Evidence confidence score 7+ | REQUIRED | From the Story Dossier |
| Enough content for 1,500+ words | REQUIRED | Not padding — genuinely enough substance to fill a long-form piece |
| Team involvement visible | Preferred | Shows how Axelerant works, not just what was delivered |
| Tradeoffs or alternatives considered | Preferred | Adds intellectual depth to the narrative |

If any REQUIRED criteria is not met, downgrade to Tier 2 or Tier 3.

## Tier 2 Checklist

| Criteria | Required? | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Genuine insight or clear learning | REQUIRED | Not just a milestone — must teach something |
| External relevance to at least one ICP persona | REQUIRED | Even if narrow |
| Evidence confidence score 5+ | REQUIRED | From the Story Dossier |
| Connected to a specific challenge or decision | REQUIRED | Not a vague observation |
| Would sustain 800-1,200 words | PREFERRED | If less, it is probably Tier 3 |

Common Tier 2 scenarios:
- A specific technical decision within a larger project (e.g., choosing a caching strategy)
- A delivery challenge that was resolved (not the full project, just that moment)
- A platform insight that came from a client engagement (e.g., a specific Drupal module tradeoff)
- A design or UX decision that had measurable impact

---

## Tier 3 Checklist

| Criteria | Required? | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Real, specific signal | REQUIRED | Not generic ("great teamwork") — must be specific |
| Human or cultural moment visible | REQUIRED | A win, a lesson, a culture beat, a personality |
| Suitable for social format | REQUIRED | Should fit in a LinkedIn post — no padding needed |

Common Tier 3 scenarios:
- A team member hit a significant delivery milestone
- A quick technical fix that had an unexpected positive impact
- A client said something notably positive
- An internal process improvement worth celebrating
- A "week in the life" moment that shows Axelerant's culture

---

## Classification Conflict Resolution

**Story feels like Tier 1 but lacks nuance**: Classify as Tier 2. Generate stakeholder questions to upgrade it. Do not write a Tier 1 blog on thin evidence.

**Story has great nuance but limited external relevance**: Classify as Tier 2. Consider whether the insight can be generalized for an industry or persona audience.

**Story has both breadth and depth**: Strong Tier 1 candidate. Flag for fast-tracking.

**Evidence is contradictory or incomplete**: Never force a tier. Flag for Story Intelligence to revisit. Prefer Tier 3 (safe and publishable) over a speculative Tier 1.

**User disagrees with classification**: Always defer to user's tier selection after showing the justification. They know the story better than the system does.
